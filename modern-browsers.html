Modern Web Browsers (1995-present)

From Browser Wars to competition and innovation

Netscape Navigator (1994)

One of the first widely used web browsers, known for its user-friendly interface and support for early web standards.

Internet Explorer (1995)

Microsoft's web browser that dominated the market for many years, introducing features like ActiveX and integrated search.

Mozilla Firefox (2002)

An open-source browser that gained popularity for its speed, security features, and extensive customization options through add-ons.

Safari (2003)

Apple's web browser, optimized for macOS and iOS, known for its clean design and energy efficiency.

Google Chrome (2008)

A fast and minimalist browser that quickly became the most popular choice, known for its performance, security, and integration with Google services.
